Faraid (Islamic Inheritance) Guide

An educational guide and example of calculating shares in Miras.

Important Disclaimer: Islamic inheritance law is highly complex. This tool is for educational purposes only and should not be used for actual estate distribution. Please consult a qualified Islamic scholar.

The Islamic law of inheritance, known as `ilm al-fara'id`, is a comprehensive and precise system outlined in the Qur'an to distribute a deceased person's estate justly among relatives. It is a core part of Islamic jurisprudence, ensuring that the wealth of a deceased Muslim is distributed according to divine guidance.

Distribution Principles

The distribution of an estate is performed in a specific order:

  1. First, all funeral expenses and any outstanding debts are settled from the estate.
  2. Next, any bequests (wasiyah) are paid, provided they do not exceed one-third of the remaining estate.
  3. The rest of the estate is then divided among the legal heirs according to their specified shares.

Types of Heirs

Heirs are primarily divided into three categories based on their relationship to the deceased:

  • Quota Heirs (`Ashab al-Faraid`): These relatives receive a fixed, specified fraction of the estate (e.g., 1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/6, 1/3, 2/3).
  • Residuaries (`Asabah`): These relatives take what is left of the estate after the Quota Heirs have received their shares. They are often the closest male relatives.
  • Distant Kindred (`Dhawi al-Arham`): These are other relatives who inherit only in the absence of Quota Heirs and Residuaries.

Illustrative Example

Below is a simple, educational example of how an estate would be divided. The rules become much more intricate with a greater number of heirs, including special cases like `Al-Awwal` and `Al-Radd`.

Scenario:

A man passes away leaving behind a total estate of $100,000. His surviving heirs are: a **Wife**, one **Son**, and one **Mother**.

Calculation:

  • Wife: She inherits the fixed share of one-eighth (1/8) due to the presence of a child.
  • Mother: She inherits the fixed share of one-sixth (1/6) due to the presence of a child.
  • Son: He is a Residuary and inherits the rest of the estate after the other fixed shares are paid.

Shares:

Wife's Share: 1/8 of $100,000 = $12,500

Mother's Share: 1/6 of $100,000 = $16,666.67

Total Quota Shares: $12,500 + $16,666.67 = $29,166.67

Remaining Estate for Son: $100,000 - $29,166.67 = $70,833.33

Final Distribution:

Wife gets: $12,500

Mother gets: $16,666.67

Son gets: $70,833.33

This is a simple case. For any real-life inheritance situation, it is crucial to seek advice from a qualified Islamic scholar or a lawyer specializing in Islamic law to ensure the distribution is performed correctly.
